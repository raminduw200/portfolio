About Me

I am a passionate Data Scientist with expertise in Computer Vision, Federated Learning, Deep Learning, and Generative AI. I hold dual degrees with First Class Honours from both the University of Colombo School of Computing (UCSC) and the University of Westminster (through IIT, Sri Lanka).

Currently working at IFS R&D International as a Data Scientist, I specialize in developing ML-based solutions including RAG systems, Time Series Forecasting, and Anomaly Detection. I have also published research in federated learning-enabled image enhancement and serve as a Visiting Lecturer at IIT.

Publications

Published

FL-CycleGAN: Enhancing Mobile Photography with Federated Learning-Enabled CycleGAN

IEEE MERCon 2024

R. Walgama and K. T. Y. Mahima

2024 Moratuwa Engineering Research Conference (MERCon), Moratuwa, Sri Lanka, pp. 688-693

Developed a secure Federated Mobile Image Enhancement method using CycleGAN. First-ever research to address distributed Image enhancement and use CycleGAN for mobile image enhancement. The proposed solution exceeds state-of-the-art models, achieving a processing speed improvement of 100x and requiring less GPU usage for processing high-resolution images. Evaluated by over 26 academic and industry experts.

IEEE Xplore

Under Review

AdvDet: Diffusion Inspired Image Watermarking Against Adversarial Attacks

ICIP 2026

R. Walgama, K.T. Yasas Mahima, Ajantha S. Atukorale

Submitted to IEEE International Conference on Image Processing 2026 (ICORE Ranking: B)

Novel adversarial input detection method inspired by diffusion models. Achieves up to 99.9% average accuracy across different attacks and above 94% accuracy across different datasets. Identifies adversarial inputs in less than half a second.

ResearchGate

Deep Learning for Audio-Visual Segmentation: A review of models, datasets, and challenges

Image and Vision Computing

Tufayl A., R. Walgama

Image and Vision Computing Journal (Impact Factor: 4.2)

Comprehensive review of Audio-Visual Segmentation (AVS) field, categorizing methodologies into four architectural paradigms. Highlights growing impact of Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) as reasoning engines.

CosSAM: Dense Audio-Visual alignment via Cosine Similarity for Audio-Visual Segmentation: A Review

ECCV 2026

Tufayl A., R. Walgama

The 18th European Conference on Computer Vision (ICORE Ranking: A*)

CosSAM contextualizes VGGish audio embeddings with visual evidence using stacked cross-attention audio-query adapters, then computes patch-wise cosine similarity against frozen DINOv3 features to form spatially dense prompts for a SAM mask decoder for audio.
